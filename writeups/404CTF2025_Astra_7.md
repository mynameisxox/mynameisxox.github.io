This challenge is part of the introduction challenges for the 404CTF (which I did not participate in). I completed the introduction challenges and number 7 was particularly enriching, so I felt compelled to write a writeup.

---

We are given an audio file to analyze. From the very first listen, it becomes clear that it's an encoded radio signal that needs to be decoded. Small problem: I know absolutely nothing about radio signals.

I load the file into Audacity and display the spectrogram. After researching radio signal spectrograms, what I see seems to resemble RTTY mode: two distinct frequency bands, a visual pattern that matches what I read in my research, and a sound close enough to mislead me.

![[file-LM29jZ7we9hJE5XXX8J3SC.png]]

I jump in, convinced I've figured it out right away.
I first try minimodem, a tool I come across for translating radio signals, estimating the two bands at 2200 and 1900 Hz:
```bash
minimodem --rx rtty -M 2200 -S 1900 -f r0Bot.wav
### CARRIER 45.45 @ 2200.0 Hz ###
MMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMNMMMMMMMNZNMVMMW HNMMVMMM MMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMNZNN
MMMMMM MMMAMMAMMMAMMMMMMMMMNMMMMMMAMVMMMAMAMMMMMMMMMM
MMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMMM
### NOCARRIER ndata=155 confidence=3.237 ampl=0.478 bps=46.36 (2.0% fast) ###
```

The result is nonsense. But rather than questioning the mode, I convince myself that my shift value is wrong. I go in circles, try other values, other modes, automatic decoding... Nothing conclusive.

I then try FLdigi, a piece of software whose interface can make you lose your eyesight — literally hostile to human beings (see for yourself below).

![[20260313140421.png]]

Once again, nothing but noise. I fight against the tool as much as against the signal for a good hour, then decide to take a break.

I come back to it the next day.
New day, motivation restored. I pick up FLdigi again with more patience this time, and start to get a slightly better grasp of the interface. But even so, I can't extract anything coherent. I decide to start my research from scratch.

That's when I come across a [YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l_3tUNRHsGI) whose audio sounds remarkably similar to what I have. I feel like this time, I'm on the right track.

I boot up Kali, install the necessary packages, and follow along with the video. A few technical issues later, and after restarting the listening session a second time because the first attempt yielded nothing, I finally get a result.

![[20260313140420.png]]

It was an image of a QR code, transmitted by the radio signal in SSTV mode.

![[R36_20250413_193552.png]]
